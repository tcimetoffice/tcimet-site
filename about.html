About Us

The Meteorology Department was established in 2021 under the Turks and Caicos Islands Airports Authority (TCIAA), with the responsibility for the monitoring of meteorological events and provision of meteorological information.

The Meteorology Department, with the assistance of the Caribbean Meteorological Organisation (CMO) and the World Meteorological Organisation (WMO), recently developed a Strategic Plan for National Meteorological Services for the Turks and Caicos Islands (2023-2027). The Strategic Plan outlines the strategic goals and objectives for the Turks and Caicos Islands National Weather Service (TCINWS) to strengthen and streamline their capacity and systems for weather, water, climate, and ocean services for enhanced decision making.

Meteorological Observers

In meteorology the understanding of the weather fundamentally stems from an accurate recording or monitoring of the current weather conditions. Meteorological Observers are tasked with this important duty; they record the weather conditions on an hourly basis and transmit this data internationally for:

  • Aviation related purposes
  • Numerical Weather Prediction Models (NWP)
  • General public

Over time, this hourly weather data can be compiled into monthly, yearly averages and extremes; and when reviewed over a 30-year (climatological average), these observations can highlight the changes in the location‚Äôs climate. If other nearby locations are also compared, a better understanding of the climate changes across the region can be gained.

Our Meteorological Observers have been trained at or by lecturers from the regional World Meteorological Organization (WMO) recognized training institution and meet the WMO basic requirement for this position.
